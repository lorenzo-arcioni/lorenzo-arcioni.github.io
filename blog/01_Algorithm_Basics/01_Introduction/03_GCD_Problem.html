Greatest Common Divisor Algorithm

Problem Definition

Develop an algorithm to compute the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two given integers. Given two non-negative integers \(a\) and \(b\) (where \(a \geq b\)), find their GCD. The GCD of two integers is the largest integer that divides both \(a\) and \(b\) without leaving a remainder.


Solution

The Euclidean algorithm is a well-known method for computing the GCD of two numbers. The algorithm is based on the principle that the GCD of two numbers also divides their difference.


The Euclidean algorithm can be mathematically represented as:


\[ GCD(a, b) = \begin{cases} |a| & \text{if } b = 0 \\ GCD(b, a \mod b) & \text{if } b \neq 0 \end{cases} \]


Real World Scenario (1)

In investment analysis, financial analysts often compare different companies using financial ratios. One common ratio is the debt-to-equity ratio, which measures a company's financial leverage. For accurate and concise reporting, it's beneficial to present these ratios in their simplest form.


Let's consider a financial analyst evaluating a company's debt-to-equity ratio:


  • The company has a total debt of $84 million.
  • The company's total equity is $120 million.

The debt-to-equity ratio can be represented as a fraction of the company's debt over its equity, which is:

\[ \frac{84}{120}. \]


Given two integers, numerator \(a\) and denominator \(b\), we want to simplify the fraction \(\frac{a}{b}\) so that the greatest common divisor (GCD) of \(a\) and \(b\) is 1. This means reducing the fraction by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their GCD.


Find GCD using the Euclidean Algorithm:

  • Let \( a = 84 \)
  • Let \( b = 120 \)
  • Compute \( 120 \mod 84 = 36 \)
  • Compute \( 84 \mod 36 = 12 \)
  • Compute \( 36 \mod 12 = 0 \)
  • Since the remainder is now 0, the GCD is 12.

Simplify the Fraction:

  • \( \text{GCD}(84, 120) = 12 \)
  • Simplified numerator: \( \frac{84}{12} = 7 \)
  • Simplified denominator: \( \frac{120}{12} = 10 \)
  • Simplified fraction: \( \frac{7}{10} \)

The simplified debt-to-equity ratio is \( \frac{7}{10} \) (or 70%), indicating that for every \$10 of equity, the company has \$7 of debt. This simplified ratio provides a clearer understanding of the company's financial leverage.


Real World Scenario (2)

You are... What is the minimum number of square towels of equal dimension that can be cut from a ream of cloth \(20\) meteres in length and \(16\) meters wide without wasting any cloth?


Solution

The measurement of one side of required square towel is \(GCD(20,16) = 4\) meters. The area of one square towel is \(4^2 = 16 m^2\). The area of full ream \(= 20 \cdot 16 = 320 m^2\). Minimum number of towels possible is: \[ \frac{320 m^2}{16 m^2} = 20 \text{ towels}. \]
